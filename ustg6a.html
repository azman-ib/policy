Value Added Tax Act (UStG) § 6a Intra-Community Supply (1) An intra-Community supply (Section 4, point 1(b)) exists if the following conditions are met in connection with a supply: 1.The entrepreneur or the customer has transported or shipped the item of delivery to another Community territory. 2.the customer is a)a business owner registered for VAT purposes in another Member State who has acquired the goods for his business, b)a legal person registered for VAT purposes in another Member State who is not a taxable person or who did not acquire the goods supplied for their business, or c)in the case of delivery of a new vehicle, any other purchaser also 3.The acquisition of the goods supplied is subject to the provisions of value added tax by the recipient in another Member State. and 4.The customer within the meaning of point 2(a) or (b) has used a valid VAT identification number issued to him by another Member State vis-à-vis the entrepreneur. The goods supplied may have been processed or worked on by agents before being transported or dispatched to the rest of the Community territory. (2) The transfer of an item which is treated as a supply shall also be deemed to be an intra-Community supply (Section 3(1a)). (3) The entrepreneur must provide evidence that the conditions set out in paragraphs 1 and 2 are met. The Federal Ministry of Finance may, with the consent of the Bundesrat, determine by statutory instrument how the entrepreneur is to provide such evidence. (4) If a supplier has treated a supply as tax-exempt even though the conditions of paragraph 1 are not met, the supply shall nevertheless be regarded as tax-exempt if the claim for tax exemption is based on incorrect information provided by the customer and the supplier could not have recognized the inaccuracy of this information even with the due diligence of a prudent businessperson. In this case, the customer owes the tax that was not collected.
